Microeconomics: Text Analysis of Conceptual Inter-relations

Lena Kim

For my semester-long project for the Data Fluency experential component at Brown University, I conducted a text analysis project on Microeconomics terms and the most important concepts within this subfield. My corpus consisted of around 100 Microeconomics articles, textbooks, and info websites.

Below is the final output, a weighted graph of Economic concepts:

The graph is linked based on the amount of conceptual "explainer" words that two concepts may share. This is an index of how inter-connected two concepts may be; for example, "market" and "price" are linked together because prices of certain assets- everything from eggs to houses- influence market perceptions, market players, and the state of a country's market.
